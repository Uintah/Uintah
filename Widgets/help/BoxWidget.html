\subsection{Box Widget} \begin{figure}[ht] \hbox to \columnwidth{\hfil \epsffile{figures/Box.eps} \hfil} \caption{Box Widget Design} \label{fig:BoxDesign} \end{figure} The Box Widget consists of twelve cylinders connected in a box (3D rectangle) arrangement where the cylinders lie on the edges of the box as shown in figure~\ref{fig:BoxDesign}. In the middle of each face of the box there is a sphere. There is a resize cylinder extending from each sphere. The primary use of the Box Widget is to select a subvolume of the workspace for further manipulation (e.g. volume rendering, isosurfaces, streamlines, FEM adaption) where the faces of the widget act as orthogonal clipping planes. If the user drags a sphere, the widget is rotated about its center without changing the position of the center. If a resize handle is dragged, the appropriate face extends without changing its orientation. If a cylinder is dragged, the entire widget moves without changing its orientation.
